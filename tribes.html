The Twelve Nations

  • New England: Maine, New Hampshire, Vermont, Massachusetts, Rhode Island, Connecticut, parts of Upstate New York
  • Greater New York City: New York City metro, Long Island, New Jersey, Delaware, Maryland, Philadelphia
  • Great Lakes: parts of Upstate New York, Western Pennsylvania, Ohio, Indiana, Illinois, Michigan, Wisconsin, Minnesota, Northern Missouri, Iowa
  • Dixieland: Virginia, West Virginia, North Carolina, South Carolina, Georgia, Alabama, Mississippi, Louisiana, Arkansas, Tennessee, Kentucky, Southern Missouri (including St. Louis and Kansas City), Northern Florida
  • South Florida: Florida south of the Orlando-Tampa line
  • Greater Texas: Texas, Oklahoma
  • The Great Plains: Nebraska, Kansas, North Dakota, South Dakota, Montana, Wyoming, Idaho, Colorado
  • Deseret: Utah, Nevada, Arizona, New Mexico, Eastern California (desert regions)
  • California: Coastal and Central California (Bay Area, Sacramento Valley, Central Valley, Los Angeles Basin), Hawaii
  • The Northwest: Oregon, Washington, Northern California
  • Alaska: Alaska
  • The 12th Tribe: A supra-tribal order composed of representatives from the Twelve Nations. It exists to safeguard the covenant, oversee matters of defense, trade, diplomacy, and common law.

The organization of tribes is by no means set in stone and are open to further ideas and discussion.

How to Join or Form a Tribe

The Confederacy is still young. More information will be provided soon on how to formally join your tribe or how to gather and organize a new one where none exists.

Those who belong to nations should begin to reflect, study, and prepare. In time, each tribe will be given the tools to organize, meet, and renew the covenant within their own lands.

For now, the call is simple: remember who you are, remember where you belong, and be ready.
